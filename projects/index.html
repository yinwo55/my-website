Public Diplomacy 2019

KF FORUM OF 100

A tailored public diplomacy program engaging 100 Chinese master's and doctoral students in Korea through expert lecture series, a Jeju Island study tour, and peer networking platforms, cultivating the next generation of Korea-focused researchers in China.

Partner Institution

Korea Foundation (한국국제교류재단)

Overview

A government-sponsored public diplomacy initiative facilitated by the Korea Foundation, bringing 100 Chinese graduate students to Korea to foster people-to-people exchange and mutual understanding between Korea and China.

Objectives

  • Reduce anti-Korea sentiment among Chinese graduate students by improving their understanding of Korean politics, society, history, and culture.
  • Cultivate the next generation of Korea-focused researchers in China by providing academic and experiential foundations during their time abroad.
  • Build a sustainable exchange platform to keep alumni engaged even after returning to China.

My Role

  • Led program planning, scheduling, and coordination across multiple stakeholders including the Korea Foundation, university partners, and external lecturers.
  • Managed a total program budget of KRW 60,000,000 (~$45,000 USD).
  • Oversaw participant recruitment, screening, and communications via WeChat (Chinese messenger app).
  • Authored the post-program results report, synthesizing program outcomes and participant feedback into a structured document submitted to the Korea Foundation.

Impact

  • Strengthened Korea's public diplomacy presence among a high-influence segment of the Chinese student community.
  • Provided a replicable model for graduate-level foreign student engagement programs.
  • Created an alumni network of emerging Korea specialists positioned to carry positive perceptions of Korea back to China.

Public Diplomacy 2021

KF Public Diplomacy University Support Program

A university-level curriculum initiative developing specialized public diplomacy education focused on East Asia, Korea-China relations, and digital diplomacy

Partner Institution

  • Korea Foundation (한국국제교류재단)
  • Sungkyunkwan University (한국 성균관대학교)

Overview

Planned and coordinated a one-year KF-funded initiative to develop and deliver specialized public diplomacy courses at the graduate level, alongside a suite of extracurricular programs targeting Chinese students and East Asian scholars studying in Korea. The program aimed to establish Sungkyunkwan University as a leading institution for East Asia-specialized public diplomacy education, integrating academic coursework with field-based and digital diplomacy experiences.

Objectives

  • Develop a Korea-model public diplomacy curriculum tailored to East Asian geopolitical and cultural contexts
  • Offer graduate-level courses on East Asian public diplomacy and Korea-China comparative public diplomacy
  • Strengthen public diplomacy awareness among foreign students (particularly Chinese graduate students) studying in Korea
  • Build a student-facing support platform (Student Success Center) for academic counseling and career development
  • Connect students with real-world public diplomacy practitioners through expert lectures and field visits

Program Structure

  • Two graduate courses developed and delivered:
    • Understanding East Asian Culture and Public Diplomacy (Fall 2021, 3 credits)
    • Korea-China Comparative Public Diplomacy Research (Spring 2022, 3 credits)
  • Extracurricular programs:
    • Joint Korea regional field trips for foreign and Korean students
    • Distinguished Lecture Series featuring public diplomacy experts
    • East Asian Graduate Student Public Diplomacy Forum
    • Public diplomacy info session for elite Chinese students in Korea
    • Student Success Center for counseling and KF program promotion

My Role

  • Coordinated program planning across multiple departments and research institutes within Sungkyunkwan University
  • Managed a total budget of KRW 30,000,000 (~$23,000 USD)
  • Oversaw recruitment, scheduling, and logistics for all extracurricular programs
  • Supported course development and liased between faculty, guest lecturers, and institutional partners

Impact

  • Established a replicable model for university-level public diplomacy education in Korea
  • Created structured engagement channels between Korean academia and Chinese student communities
  • Contributed to KF's broader goal of cultivating the next generation of public diplomacy practitioners in East Asia

Policy Research 2021

Korea-China Policy Research Series

A two-round policy dialogue series on U.S.-China competition, Northeast Asian security, and bilateral relations

Partner Institution

Korea Foundation (한국국제교류재단)

Overview

Coordinated a two-round bilateral research forum bringing together 20 Korean and Chinese policy experts to comparatively analyze shifts in U.S.-China strategic competition, Northeast Asian security, and the future of Korea-China relations. The series culminated in a co-authored publication released in early 2022 to mark the 30th anniversary of Korea-China diplomatic normalization.

Objectives

  • Compare Korean and Chinese expert perspectives on shared policy challenges across diplomacy, security, and economic restructuring
  • Analyze the implications of the Biden administration's foreign policy for Northeast Asian security architecture
  • Examine global value chain realignment and its impact on Korea-China economic cooperation
  • Introduce China's emerging "Chinese Path" discourse to Korean academic and policy audiences
  • Produce a bilingual policy publication as a knowledge public good for scholars and policymakers

My Role

  • Managed end-to-end planning and execution of two online research forums with simultaneous Korean-Chinese interpretation
  • Coordinated contributions from 20 experts across both countries, overseeing manuscript collection, translation, and editorial review
  • Managed a total budget of KRW 35,000,000 (~$27,000 USD)
  • Oversaw publication production including editing, translation of 24 papers, and print/e-book formatting

Impact

  • Delivered a structured Track II civilian cooperation platform between Korean and Chinese policy communities
  • Published a commemorative volume tentatively titled Korea's Path and China's Path, marking 30 years of bilateral diplomatic relations
  • Strengthened ongoing Korea-China expert networks and laid groundwork for future joint research initiatives

Policy Research 2021

Korea's Foreign Strategy in a Changing World Order

A commissioned multi-author policy research project analyzing Korea's grand strategy amid U.S.-China competition, alliance restructuring, and shifting Northeast Asian security architecture

Partner Institution

Korea Institute for International Economic Policy (대외경제정책연구원, KIEP)

Overview

Coordinated a multi-author policy research project examining how South Korea should reorient its foreign strategy in an era of accelerating U.S.-China rivalry, liberal international order decline, and alliance uncertainty. The project brought together six leading Korean IR scholars to produce a comprehensive 7-chapter volume covering the full spectrum of Korea's strategic environment, from the U.S.-led order and alliance politics to China policy, Japan relations, and new regional horizons beyond Northeast Asia.

Objectives

  • Diagnose the structural limitations of Korea's existing foreign policy frameworks rooted in path-dependent liberal order assumptions
  • Develop a forward-looking Korean grand strategy concept suited to Korea's rising middle power status
  • Analyze the decline of U.S.-led liberal international order and its implications for Korean diplomacy
  • Assess alliance restructuring options amid intensifying U.S.-China competition on the Korean Peninsula
  • Formulate Korea's China strategy in response to Beijing's increasingly assertive regional posture
  • Explore multi-track Japan strategy and Korea's diplomatic horizon beyond the Korean Peninsula and Northeast Asia

My Role

  • Served as contract coordinator between KIEP and Sungkyunkwan University
  • Managed administrative and logistical support for the research team of 6 co-investigators
  • Coordinated manuscript collection, interim reporting, and final report submission
  • Managed a total budget of KRW 31,000,000 (~$24,000 USD)

Impact

  • Produced a policy-relevant framework for Korea's proactive grand strategy at a pivotal moment in Northeast Asian geopolitics
  • Contributed to KIEP's ongoing research agenda on Korea's foreign economic and diplomatic strategy
  • Advanced academic and policy discourse on Korea's strategic autonomy amid great power competition
Policy Research 2022

Korea-China Future Relations Action Plan

A Ministry of Foreign Affairs-commissioned policy research project developing concrete action plans for the Korea-China Future Development Committee, translating bilateral policy recommendations into implementable roadmaps across diplomacy, trade, society, and maritime cooperation.

Policy Research 2022

Korea-China Future Relations Action Plan

A Ministry of Foreign Affairs-commissioned project developing implementable roadmaps for the Korea-China Future Development Committee

Partner Institution

Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Republic of Korea (대한민국 외교부)

Overview

Supported a Ministry of Foreign Affairs policy research project tasked with translating the bilateral policy recommendations of the Korea-China Future Development Committee into concrete, implementable action plans. The project analyzed proposals submitted by both the Korean and Chinese delegations of the Committee's Future Planning Subcommittee, covering seven Korean policy recommendations and six Chinese proposals, and developed prioritized roadmaps across political-diplomatic, economic-trade, sociocultural, and maritime cooperation domains. Research was conducted over five months and delivered as a final policy report to the Korean government ahead of the 30th anniversary of Korea-China diplomatic normalization.

Objectives

  • Analyze and evaluate policy recommendations submitted by both Korean and Chinese sides of the Future Development Committee
  • Develop actionable implementation plans aligned with the new Korean government's foreign policy direction
  • Build consensus between Korean and Chinese policy proposals across key bilateral cooperation areas
  • Assess China's maritime cooperation proposals and develop Korea-centered response strategies
  • Produce a comprehensive policy roadmap to guide Korea-China relations for the next 30 years

My Role

  • Assisted in research coordination, literature review, and data collection across five months
  • Supported expert consultation scheduling and advisory session management
  • Contributed to drafting and editing of policy analysis sections and action plan components
  • Handled administrative operations and budget management
  • Managed a total budget of KRW 30,000,000 (~$23,000 USD)

Impact

  • Delivered a comprehensive policy report to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs supporting the Korea-China Future Development Committee's final recommendations
  • Contributed to Korea's diplomatic preparation for the 30th anniversary of Korea-China normalization
  • Provided a structured bilateral consensus-building framework applicable to future Track 1.5 dialogue processes

Corporate Geopolitical Analysis 2021

China Current Affairs Intelligence Report

A year-long corporate intelligence partnership delivering bi-monthly China current affairs reports to Amorepacific Group

Partner Institution

AMORE PACIFIC (아모레퍼시픽)

Overview

Supported a year-long corporate research partnership delivering bi-monthly China current affairs intelligence reports to Amorepacific Group. The project provided systematic, multi-disciplinary analysis of China's political, economic, social, and cultural dynamics, tracking macro-level strategic shifts alongside micro-level developments to support Amorepacific's business strategy in the Chinese market. Reports were delivered twice monthly, covering China's grand strategy, Party Congress developments, regulatory changes, and emerging market trends.

Objectives

  • Monitor real-time developments in Chinese politics, economics, and society through systematic analysis of official statements, policy documents, and expert discourse
  • Track strategic implications of China's "Common Prosperity" policy and Xi Jinping's potential third term following the 20th Party Congress
  • Analyze shifts in China's industrial and regulatory environment relevant to corporate operations
  • Provide forward-looking intelligence on China's future policy trajectory using interdisciplinary social science perspectives
  • Deliver timely, accessible analysis to support evidence-based corporate decision-making

My Role

  • Conducted ongoing current affairs analysis and monitoring of Chinese government statements, policy documents, and expert commentary
  • Compiled and synthesized bi-monthly intelligence reports delivered to Amorepacific Group
  • Managed research coordination, expert consultation scheduling, and administrative operations
  • Managed a total budget of KRW 35,000,000 (~$27,000 USD)

Impact

  • Delivered 24 bi-monthly intelligence reports over a 12-month period
  • Provided Amorepacific Group with timely, policy-grounded analysis of China's evolving regulatory and market landscape
  • Contributed to corporate strategic planning at a critical juncture marked by China's post-COVID recovery and major political transition

Policy Research 2021

30 Years of Korea-China Relations: Achievements and Vision

A commissioned expert survey and policy research project assessing 30 years of Korea-China diplomatic relations

Partner Institution

Korea Institute for International Economic Policy (대외경제정책연구원, KIEP)

Overview

Supported a commissioned research project examining the state of Korea-China relations on the 30th anniversary of bilateral diplomatic normalization, through a jointly designed expert perception survey administered simultaneously in both countries. The study surveyed 200 specialists (100 Korean China experts and 100 Chinese Korea experts) across political, diplomatic, economic, and sociocultural dimensions, using a bilingual questionnaire developed collaboratively by Korean and Chinese researchers.

Objectives

  • Assess the current state of Korea-China mutual perceptions among policy experts across politics, economics, and society
  • Identify key drivers behind perception gaps and image formation between Korean and Chinese specialist communities
  • Build on prior longitudinal survey infrastructure to track shifts in bilateral expert perceptions over time
  • Generate evidence-based insights to support Korea-China public diplomacy programming on the 30th anniversary
  • Contribute to KIEP's 2021 Korea-China Future Vision Forum with empirical perception data

My Role

  • Served as research assistant, supporting data collection, respondent outreach, and administrative coordination
  • Assisted in coordinating the Chinese-side survey through local research coordinators
  • Supported translation, data coding, and processing of survey responses
  • Assisted with literature review, interim reporting, and final report preparation
  • Managed a total budget support of KRW 35,000,000 (~$27,000 USD)

Impact

  • Delivered empirical perception data marking the 30th anniversary of Korea-China diplomatic normalization
  • Advanced the longitudinal survey methodology established in the 2020 pilot study
  • Contributed to KIEP's Korea-China Future Vision Forum research agenda
  • Provided a replicable bilateral expert survey framework for future annual tracking studies
